On-Beat UX - UI Design

OnBeat is a conceptual app that works with a wearable watch extension to track your heart on the go. I was inspired to do this after my mother suffered a heart attack.

Doctors couldn’t identify why she was randomly having chest pains from time to time. One of Onbeat’s functionality is to track your heart, on the go. When the wearable detects any irregular pulse, a module will pop up, and ask you to fill in the input.

This data can be shared with your doctor for better assessment. The UI is very clean and easy to use. The app is meant for all users, even elders.

Chips and Dairy Rebranding

This was a re-branding assignment of a local business in Ottawa, Ontario. I chose ‘Chips and Dairy’, a Canadian/Chinese restaurant.

The challenge I faced was how to show the identity of both cultures in the logo of the restaurant. Therefore, I went with a very clean western type face, while the ampersand sign mimics a soup bowl with chopsticks. Thus representing the Asian culture.

The outcome was very successful, as it shows both the Chinese and Canadian cultures in the brand, yet very simple.

My Wordmark

My wordmark is inspired from my Canadian/Arabic background. This is a very unique style that has not been done so far with a mix of both, Arabic and English.

The wordmark comes together to create my first and last name. Although it’s unique, I find that it is hard for individuals who can only read english to grasp on to it. People who read Arabic and English grasp on to the concept very quickly.
